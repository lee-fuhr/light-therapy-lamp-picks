Light therapy lamps have become a go-to tool for managing seasonal affective disorder (SAD) and regulating sleep cycles. The basic idea: you sit near a lamp that emits bright, full-spectrum light (typically 10,000 lux at a specific distance) for about 20–30 minutes each morning. But not all lamps are created equal. The best ones offer adjustable brightness, a large enough panel to bathe your face in light, and a color temperature that mimics natural daylight. When shopping, look for a lamp that provides at least 10,000 lux at a comfortable distance (most effective lamps do this at 12–18 inches), has a UV-free design, and tilts or adjusts to direct the light where you need it.
