Quick answer

For most people on a budget, the Circadian Optics Lumine is the best light therapy lamp, it’s compact, very affordable, and delivers the 10,000 lux intensity you need without any fuss.

Light therapy lamps, also called SAD lights, are one of the most effective non‑medication tools for combating seasonal affective disorder and the winter blues. The core idea is simple: you sit near a lamp that emits 10,000 lux of cool-white or full‑spectrum light for about 20–30 minutes each morning. That exposure helps regulate your circadian rhythm and serotonin levels, which can boost mood and energy when daylight is scarce. Choosing a budget model doesn’t mean sacrificing effectiveness, but you do need to look for a few key features. The lamp should be large enough to deliver 10,000 lux at a comfortable distance (usually 12–18 inches), have an adjustable brightness or at least a solid fixed output, and be easy to position on a desk or table top. UV‑free operation is standard for reputable brands. The three picks below all meet those criteria while staying budget‑friendly, so you can find the right fit without overspending.
